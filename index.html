Let's visualize the process. The plot shows a cost function, our starting point, and the path taken by gradient descent. Notice how the steps get smaller as we approach the minimum.

Stop and Think

In our blindfolded valley analogy, what would happen if you took very large steps? What if you took very tiny steps?

Very large steps might lead you to stumble and overshoot the lowest point, potentially even climbing up another hill! Very tiny steps would ensure you're always going downhill, but it might take you a very long time to reach the bottom.
